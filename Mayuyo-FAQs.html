Q1 — What is the "Mars Science Helicopter" (MSH)?
A1 — The Mars Science Helicopter (MSH) is a second-generation conceptual rotorcraft for Mars designed to perform independent science investigations (instead of being just a technology demonstrator). The conceptual designs started from coaxial and hexacopter configurations, aiming for a science payload around 2–3 kg and a vehicle mass near 20 kg.
Source: NASA TM 2020-220485 — Mars Science Helicopter Conceptual Design (PDF)
Q2 — What design constraints make flying on Mars different from Earth?
A2 — The Martian atmosphere is much thinner, producing very low Reynolds numbers and unique compressibility effects at rotor tip speeds. That means airfoils and rotor designs must be optimized for low-Re flow, structures must be extremely lightweight, and packaging (to fit inside aeroshells/landers) constrains rotor size.
Source: NASA TM 2020-220485 (PDF)
Q3 — What configurations were considered and what was the Mars Helicopter (Ingenuity)?
A3 — The conceptual study began with coaxial and hexacopter layouts. The Mars Helicopter technology demonstrator (often called Ingenuity) is a coaxial vehicle with mass ≈1.8 kg and rotor diameter ≈1.21 m; it uses solar + battery power and was designed for short autonomous flights (~90 seconds per flight) as a technology demonstration.
Source: NASA TM 2020-220485 (PDF)
Q4 — What mission performance targets were set for MSH?
A4 — Typical design targets included a 2–3 kg science payload, extended range on the order of 2–4 km, and hover times of roughly 2–4 minutes to enable meaningful science operations beyond simple short flights. The vehicle design target mass to meet these goals is on the order of 20 kg.
Source: NASA TM 2020-220485 (PDF)
Q5 — Are there concrete example performance numbers from the study?
A5 — Yes. One example in the study shows a conceptual 31 kg hexacopter that fits within a 2.5 m aeroshell, which could carry a ~5 kg payload for about 10 minutes of hover or span a range of roughly 5 km (design dependent).
Source: NASA TM 2020-220485 (PDF)
