Overview

The Mars Science Helicopter (MSH) is a second-generation rotorcraft concept designed for scientific exploration on Mars. Unlike the Ingenuity helicopter (which was a technology demonstrator), MSH is aimed at carrying larger science payloads and operating independently to expand Mars exploration capabilities.

Key Highlights

  • Purpose: Conduct real science missions, not just technology demonstration.

  • Constraints: Thin atmosphere (low density), packaging inside aeroshells, need for lightweight but strong structures, and efficient rotors for low-Reynolds-number flow.

Example Concept

One design in the study showed a 31 kg hexacopter that could carry a ~5 kg payload, hover for ~10 minutes, or cover ~5 km range while still fitting into a 2.5 m aeroshell.

Conclusion

The Mars Science Helicopter shows strong potential for extending robotic exploration of Mars by reaching areas inaccessible to rovers and landers, providing valuable science data with aerial mobility.
